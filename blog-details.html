How RTK GPS Positioning Enhances Flight Resolution

Marcus Vance Chief Technical Officer | June 15, 2026

Standard commercial GPS systems offer a positioning accuracy of roughly 2 to 5 meters. While adequate for high-altitude single drone photography, this margin is far too dangerous for complex swarming formations, where drones fly as close as 1.2 meters from each other.

Understanding RTK (Real-Time Kinematic)

RTK technology solves this limitation by introducing a local reference base station on the ground. This base station receives the same satellite signals as the drone fleet in the air. Since the base station's position on Earth is fixed and precisely calculated beforehand, it computes carrier-phase measurements in real-time, sending error correction telemetry directly to the drone fleet via localized radio bands.

"By employing multi-constellation RTK correction data, our drone systems achieve horizontal precision of under 2 centimeters, allowing for razor-sharp text and intricate 3D animations."

Safety Implications

Aside from artistic precision, centimeter-level coordinate tracking serves as a foundation for flight safety. In the event of sudden atmospheric wind changes, the onboard autopilot corrects drift instantly, avoiding neighboring flight paths. If the RTK link drops, the system instantly switches back to standard GNSS, increasing drone separation automatically.
